How to use this tool

Click 'BEGIN THE ASSESSMENT' to start. Navigate using the 'NEXT' button on each page.
For each section, answer 2 to 5 questions using dropdown menus to select the option that best fits your knowledge.
Select answers based on where your organisation is now and where it wants to be in the future (3-5 years).
You will receive a level of maturity score and a tailored action plan to progress between levels.
Scored from Level 1 Observer to Level 5 Innovator.

Full Reference Model Overview
L1: Observer L2: Early Adopter L3: Effective Deliverer L4: Leader L5: Innovator
Overview Dimensions Descriptions Level 1 Observer Level 2 Early Adopter Level 3 Effective Deliverer Level 4 Leader Level 5 Innovator
Policy and Funding Policy Organisational policy influence on the ability to deliver PH/ULE social housing. No policy commitment or strategic alignment with PH/ULE goals. Policy shows early interest or in development. Policy supports PH/ULE delivery. Policy sets high standards with strategic alignment and formal mechanisms. Policy leads on housing innovation, shaping national agendas.
Funding Ability to attract, secure, and influence funding sources beyond conventional routes. No resource or strategy to secure funding for PH/ULE. Plans to recruit resources and seek support for suitable funding options. Has business model and successfully accessed non-standard funding. In-house specialists and regular diverse funding streams. Influence the funder and funding model; advocate for increased funding.
Overarching Strategies Delivery strategy Structured approach to effectively implement PH/ULE delivery. No structured strategy. Implementation is ad hoc or isolated pilots. Early planning exists but lacks consistency or integration. Structured delivery strategy in place that led to isolated success. Strategy is embedded and reviewed regularly. Processes are replicable. Strategy drives innovation and informs sector best practice.
Quality assurance Ensuring PH/ULE projects consistently meet performance and quality outcomes. QA is ad hoc, reactive, and mostly dependent on external requirements. Basic QA processes exist but are inconsistently applied. QA strategy is structured and applied in some cases. QA is embedded. Continuous improvement driven by audits. Sector-leading; predictive QA and third-party certification.
Procurement Strategic, value-driven approach supporting policy objectives. Procurement is basic; no resources for procurement strategy. Some consideration of energy performance in procurement. Established procurement strategy for small-scale delivery. Strategic procurement is embedded. Partnerships used for scale. Pioneers innovative models that drive sector transformation.
Organisational Capability Leadership & Gov Dedicated team with clear leadership and defined roles. No dedicated leadership or governance structure. Roles/responsibilities emerging. Informal tracking of progress. Clear leadership and governance structure in place. Governance is embedded. Team has authority and resources. Champions PH/ULE at strategic level; shaping culture.
Training & Skills Ability to identify and support structured training programs. No or low level of knowledge. Minimal investment in training. Basic awareness and informal learning; limited in scope. Team has PH/ULE experience. Structured training in place. Knowledgeable/experienced team. Active investment in skill-building. Co-develops training with partners to lead sector-wide skills.
Stakeholder Eng. Ensuring strategic and operational backing for implementation. Limited or reactive engagement. Initial efforts to engage stakeholders. Stakeholder engagement is structured and ongoing. Strong partnerships and co-ownership. Active contributions. Co-creates strategies; builds alliances influencing sector adoption.
Process Maturity Post Occupancy Structured plans for Soft Landings framework and tenant education. No formal post-occupancy strategy. Limited engagement. Basic handover support; limited tenant feedback collection. Structured Soft Landings plan; some liaison processes in place. Clear engagement strategy; proactive education and feedback. Fully integrated post-occupancy framework; sector-leading.
Monitoring Enable continuous improvement through feedback loops (POE/real-time). No structured monitoring. Performance data not collected. Some tools used in isolated projects. Data is limited. POE and real-time monitoring implemented across projects. Uses centralised digital systems and pattern books. Leads in data-driven POE. Analytics inform sector learning.
Supply Chain Readiness Engagement Influencing suppliers' willingness to deliver PH/ULE projects. Not engaged with supply chain; minimal influence. Some dialogue; limited influence on understanding. Actively engages suppliers with PH/ULE requirements. Builds strategic relationships; suppliers are aligned. Suppliers co-drive innovation and promote adoption.
Joint Innovation Capacity to innovate in methods, materials, and prefabrication. No innovation encouraged; conventional approaches dominate. Some interest; limited access to advanced components. Supports suppliers in adopting improved methods. Incentivises innovation in prefabrication and efficiency. Co-creates new solutions/prototypes with suppliers.
Integration and Scalability SC Integration Align procurement and delivery through coordinated frameworks (ECI). Minimal coordination; operate in isolation. Some coordination; ECI is rare or informal. Partially aligned; ECI used in some projects. Coordinated frameworks in place; strong partnerships. Leads in integrated delivery; sector-leading standard tools.
Scalability Scaling up from pilots to multiple consistent projects. Not delivered any PH/ULE project. Delivers isolated projects; ad hoc and reactive. Begins to replicate pilot success. Planning emerging. Capable of delivering multiple projects reliably. Expanding capacity systematically; setting benchmarks.
